Three Examples

Five years ago, mob programming was something we hadn’t heard about at ctm. Today, most teams have tried it and many have found that it helps them.

Some have been doing it for years, some are still new to it.

I’m going to tell the story of three teams that started mobbing What went well, what went not so well What was learned

I’m hoping that these will encourage you to give mobbing a go, or if you already have, find ways to make your mobs even better.

Unsuitable 😴

Inflexible 🙉

Too Crowded 😕

not all stories make sense to mob on e.g. when we need to do investigation e.g. when it’s “easy” or “tedious”

Team needed more specialists, so grew. Mobs became too big, disengaged.

Doing multiple things at once sometimes makes sense

Demob

(di•mob, verb)

💬 "Hmm, not sure how to fix this. Shall we demob and do some research?"

💬 "Someone from another team wants to use our new library. I'll demob and help them."

Mobabble

(mobb•a•ble, adj)

💬 "This story is mobabble, because we'll need to refactor quite a lot of the code-base."

💬 "This just needs finishing off now, I don't think it's mobbable anymore."

Right Team For (Each) Job

“We had a hammer, so everything looked like a nail" Mobbing is a great tool, but not always the right tool for the job Be explicit that you're demobbing to help others.
